Why have I chosen to come here?

I started to code on my own thanks to CodeCademy and FreeCodeCamp at work. The school is very supportive and I feel like I have finally found what I want to do in my life.

My idea of a project:

There are three main steps to follow in order to effectively start a project:

  1. Describing:

    2. First, let's identify the purposes of IT in modern business:

    • Communication;
    • Accountability;
    • Organisation.

    IT provides the tools needed for companies or individuals to successfully run a business. With the advent of IT, organisations are finally able to keep up with the ever-changing world of technology and innovation and can excel at their tasks, wherever they are based.

  2. Planning:

    3. Second, the planning of a project entails more than just mind maps and graphs. Since a project is delivered by people, it is recommended that each member meets each other and is aware of their past projects and strongest skills.

    Then,

    • Goals and desired outcomes are identified;
    • A delivery schedule is drafted according to a set timescale;
    • Various portions of the budget are allocated to micro-projects according to urgency.

  3. Selecting:

    4. Finally, Identifying the goals in regard to the desired outcome of your project is paramount when trying to select the right kind of technology for it. Three principal actions are required:

    • Research;
    • Sort;
    • Test.

    Before establishing what the appropriate technology your project may need the most, it is essential to research the market. In that way, we are more conscious of what the needs of the market are, and whether it is worth investing a certain amount of energy and funds in a project. After sorting out the finances and realising how much we are able to invest, and specifically in what, we can select what platforms to first test our product and get feedback to see whether the investment might me worth it.


How I would continue a project:

Again, I have three main steps to follow in order to effectively carry on with a project:

  1. Methods:
    2. A pre-tested business strategy; budget management analysis; real-time reporting or editing.

  2. Skills:
    3. Project management; organisation; communication; time-keeping; accuracy.

  3. Resources:
    4. A strong internet connection; access to cloud-sharing platforms; to-do lists.

  4. Tools:
    5. Laptops; internet; spreadsheets; tables; graphs; software like Trello.


How I would explain a project:

  1. Analysing:

    2. The rationale behind the choice of certain technologies over others is to be explained a posteriori. Examples may be:

    • Budget restrictions;
    • Emphasis on aspects vs others (e.g. speed over aesthetics);
    • The need to run a program on specific platforms only.

  2. Preventing:

    3. Studies have showed that the digital technology industry has a success rate below 60%. What causes digital projects to fail? Reasons, amongst others, are:

    • Limited digital strategy or access to digital resources;
    • Inadequate training or experience of team members;
    • Mismatch of skills.

  3. GDPR and privacy:

    4. The EU General Data Protection Regulation (known as GDPR) is a legal object designed to protect the personal data of individuals. Whilst undoubtedly important, GDPR could pose constraints on digital projects. For instance, it may:

    • Impact the freedom and convenience of cloud-sharing platforms that are useful while working on the same project from different locations;
    • Require businesses to invest more in ensuring that their compliance is on point, with this swaying funds from other projects;
    • Slow down the execution of new projects because of legal intricacies.


    Project review and improvements


    Review of tools:

    In making my own website I predominantly used Trello, Visual Studio Code, and Google Chrome, in this order. The first is useful to draft and visualize the content of the website, the second in that I was able to test HTML, JavaScript and CSS codes simultaneously, and the last in order to see the product of my work.

    The strengths of these IT tools have been:

    • Visualizing the contents and mind-mapping my project beforehand;
    • The opportunity to test my code on three different pages;
    • Using the Developers' tools to see the different sections of the page and quickly being able to know what to edit in my code.

    However, some weaknesses have been:

    • The fact that I had to save my code and refresh the page to see changes;
    • The limited use of the 'magical' JavaScript;
    • The not-so-much dynamism or flowiness of the page.


    In terms of future improvements, the idea is to incorporate further elements in the CSS.style document in order to improve the styling and to learn how to make the most of JavaScript.


    Copyright and websites

    What Why How
    The copyright is a form of intellectual property law. In order to protect the author's rights when it comes to reproducing his ideas without their explicit permission. The author does not have to apply or pay any fee in order to be copyright-protected. Thanks to copyright, the author us protected by the law in that others are not allowed to copy, distribute or spread the author's work.

    Cookies and privacy

    What Why How
    Cookies are text files and not programs. They contain a site name and an unique user ID. As a marketing strategy by the website host so that they know how many times you have visited their website and they are able to tailor their content and advertisement more accurately. Every time you visit a website, a cookie is downloaded onto your computer or device. They are then able to record most of your browsing habits, hence the recent controversy related to privacy and the GDPR guidelines.
